FAQ

What are shared resources?

Your website shares the memory, computing power and bandwidth of the servers it resides on with others. Another website cannot access your website or the resources it occupies.

We set limits based on the web hosting offer you choose and can increase them if we see your website may need a little extra.

Bandwidth is shared and we throttle it to make sure everybody get's a fair share. If we see your website constantly needs a lot more, we will advise you and you are free to remain on the plan you have chosen.

If you have chosen the 'Dedicated Performance' plan, your websites will run on a virtual private server with dedicated CPU, RAM, IP address and bandwidth allocation.

What is standard support?

We provide support via email only for standard support and you can expect a response within one hour during normal UK business hours (9am-5pm GMT, Mon-Fri). Our dedicated offer provides support options through a Service Level Agreement tailored to your needs.

What about RAM, CPU and traffic?

We have listened to our customers over the years and they told us that they find figures provided not representative of the actual performance or quality of service. So we decided to not provide them anymore. We set base limits for resources and change them based on individual websites needs. 

For smaller websites with lesser visitors, the standard performance works well and saves money. Larger sites or those with more visitors may want larger amounts of resources and bandwidth to get the same good performance level.

You can change your hosting plan at any time up or down.

How do you compare with other web hosters?

Other managed website hosting companies will, in most cases, not set up or amend your domain names, email accounts or website software. You will get access to a control panel and knowledge base and some technical support. If you want them to do something for you, you will usually have to pay extra for a 'white gloves' concierge-style hosting service.

You will not need to login to anything or worry about configuration and settings, we will do these things for you.

Where do I log-in?

Nowhere. There are no log-ins, no registration screens and we positively won't ask you to read any knowledge base articles or open a ticket.

Just say hello@bebraver.uk, using plain English and tell us what you need. From registering domains to making staging sites go live safely or restoring something from backup - we do it for you by email.

Are you GDPR compliant?

We take your privacy seriously and do not store data beyond the intended purpose. For example, your billing information is kept in our billing system and payments are taken using PCI compliant gateways using the information only for the transaction needed.

When you email us, we will keep the details you supply so we can contact you about what you emailed us about and keep a record of the messages for future reference. You can ask us for a copy of your data at any time, free of charges and we happily oblige wiping the records. Financial transaction data and associated contact information will need to be retained for taxation purposes as required by UK legislation.

When you subscribe to our newsletter, your data will be stored and used only for the newsletter and you can remove yourself from it at any time.

Please follow our blog on Medium for more information about GDPR and privacy.
